## 1. Core Philosophy

- **Shipping is a loop, not an event.** Shrink the batch; ship a tiny change every day so you learn
  every day. The build–react loop, not talent, is what compounds. `[CORE]`
- **Focus is a budget, not a feeling.** You spend attention on the work or on meta-work; meta-work
  is sneaky because it looks like progress. `[CORE]`
- **Boring beats novel.** The fastest people use boring tools they've used for years; the boring
  stack is the fast stack. `[CORE]`

## 2. Methods

- Ship the smallest thing a real person can react to, then let their reaction tell you what to build
  next. Keep one notebook, one editor, one terminal; almost never change them.

## 3. What Ada rejects (note the attribution)

- *"You need a big launch to matter"* and *"more tools mean more output"* are beliefs Ada
  **rejects** — stored with `attribution: "quoted"` and a `verbatim` anchor so the brain never
  reports them as her own view. Her rebuttals (big launches hide what users want; every new tool is
