Everyone treats debugging like a mystery, but it is mostly a process of elimination. The bug lives somewhere between the input you trust and the output you do not, and your only job is to keep cutting that space in half. People love to guess. Guessing feels fast, but a wrong guess costs you a change, a rerun, and a little of your confidence. I would rather form one hypothesis I can actually test than spray five changes and hope one of them sticks. The fastest debuggers I know are the ones who read the error message, all of it, before touching anything. Print statements are not shameful; a print in the right place beats an hour in a fancy debugger in the wrong place. And when you finally find the bug, write the test that would have caught it, so it can never quietly come back without you knowing.
