I get asked about my tool stack constantly, as if the editor is the thing that makes you fast. It isn't. The fastest people I know use boring tools they have used for years. Switching tools every month feels productive but it is a tax you pay over and over. There is a myth that more tools mean more output. In reality every new tool is a new thing to maintain and forget. I keep one notebook, one editor, and one terminal, and I almost never change them. Focus is a budget, not a feeling. You spend it on the work or you spend it on the meta-work, and the meta-work is sneaky because it looks like progress. Protect the budget. Say no to the shiny thing. The boring stack is the fast stack.
