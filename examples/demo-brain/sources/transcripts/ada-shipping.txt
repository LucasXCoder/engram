People always ask me what the secret to shipping software is, and honestly there isn't one secret. The thing that actually works is shrinking the batch. If you ship a tiny change every day, you learn every day. A lot of beginners think you need a big launch to matter. That's wrong. Big launches hide what users actually want behind months of guessing. I'd rather be slightly embarrassed by version one than proud of a version that never ships. The other trap is polishing. Polish is real work, but polishing something nobody uses is just procrastination with extra steps. So my rule is simple: ship the smallest thing that a real person can react to, then let their reaction tell you what to build next. That loop, not talent, is what compounds over a career.
